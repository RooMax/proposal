Introduction

Auric Entertainment (Malaysia) is honoured to present "Hadagasma ‚Äî Bathiya & Santhush Unplugged Concert in Malaysia", featuring Sri Lanka's most iconic musical duo Bathiya & Santhush (BNS).

For over two decades, BNS has redefined Sri Lankan music by blending Sinhala, Tamil, English, and Hindi influences, becoming pioneers in the South Asian music industry. Their performances have mesmerized audiences worldwide, and they are celebrated for their ability to bring together diverse communities through music.

Historic Milestone: This will be the first full BNS concert ever held in Malaysia, exclusively for the Sri Lankan and South Asian community.

Diplomatic Collaboration: This concert is proudly organized in collaboration with the Sri Lankan High Commission in Malaysia, and with our community partner Sri Lankan Workers Welfare Society (SLWWS), strengthening cultural and diplomatic ties between Sri Lanka and Malaysia.

This landmark event promises to be an unforgettable celebration of music, culture, and community, bringing together Sri Lankans and South Asian music lovers across Malaysia and Singapore.

Objectives of the Event

1. Strengthen Bilateral & Cultural Relations

  • To celebrate Sri Lanka's rich musical heritage in Malaysia and foster stronger cultural and diplomatic ties between the two nations.
  • To position Sri Lanka's modern music industry on an international platform, showcasing it to both Sri Lankans and the wider multicultural audience in Malaysia.

2. Unite the Sri Lankan Expatriate Community

  • To bring together Sri Lankans living in Malaysia and Singapore under one cultural roof.
  • To promote a sense of unity, identity, and pride within the expatriate community while keeping them connected to their homeland.

3. Create Meaningful Social Impact

  • To contribute RM 45,000 towards Apeksha Hospital (Maharagama), supporting cancer care and oncology services in Sri Lanka.
  • To demonstrate the power of cultural events in driving positive social change and community development.
  • To continue the success of previous initiatives like the Rhythms of Hope water plant project in Vavuniya.

4. Promote Youth & Student Engagement

  • To encourage participation from the younger generation through affordable ticketing options.
  • To inspire youth to engage in cultural appreciation and community building.

5. Establish Malaysia as a Cultural Hub for Sri Lankans

  • To create a landmark event that positions Malaysia as a regional hub for Sri Lankan cultural activities.
  • To lay the foundation for future cultural and musical collaborations between Sri Lankans in Malaysia and beyond.
